Page Three of

The Cursed Heirs

By Mahi Sheth


The curse took hold of the princess of Eldrathis, pulsing in the shape of dark veins spreading like cracks beneath the girl’s skin before gathering at her intricate knot, turning its color tar. “No you can’t—”

She turned around, looking at the boy. “Hush,” she said, silencing his cries. “Your turn. But you seem awfully attached to someone who’s supposed to be your enemy, we might have to do something about that.

"Your hands untouched, yet covered in blood, your crown will be a prison. The sins of your ancestors will stain every step you take, and your heart will carry the weight of their wrongs. Your actions will be shadowed by their mistakes, and your people will never see you as their savior, but as a catalyst of their suffering. Cursed because of a vow broken long before your birth, no matter how hard you try to escape, your bloodline will follow you, trapping you in the very throne you are destined to inherit. There is one, bound by darkness, whose presence alone will twist the course of your fate. But beware—you will despise her on sight, for hatred will come as easily as breath. And yet, only through love, given freely and without hesitation, can the chains be broken. What you must do will demand more than you are prepared to give, for some bonds are not so easily forged, and fewer can withstand the weight of destiny."

As the last line was spoken, the shadows released the unconscious boy. His mark glowed a bright red before dimming, solidifying his curse, as he fell to the ground.

The only question that lingered in her mind as she began erasing their memories of this event was—how did he know who she was? She had been certain she had wiped every trace of her existence from the scrolls, every record buried, every whisper silenced. And yet, he knew.

Rising to her feet, she pulled her hood back over her head, shrouding herself once more. Without a backward glance, she strode out of the throne room, the heavy doors slamming shut behind her, sealing the boy and girl in darkness
