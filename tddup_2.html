Till Death Do Us Part

By Mahi Sheth


Shadow's eyes blaze with anger as he sees Death, held against the wall, collapse to her knees as the invisible grip from his father falls, focusing on a new threat. He watches in quiet fury as her hands come around to soothe her neck.

Lucifer puts out his hands to stop the shadows, blasting a wave of hellfire, an elementonly the king of the underworld is able to have. As the shadows keep the king at bay, Shadow wraps himself in the darkness and wields it, bringing him to Death. He stretches the darkness so that it wraps around her as well and shadowwalks them to their bedroom. 

In disbelief, Shadow sets his wife on the bed. He heads to the washroom and grabs a cold, wet towel. Death stares at him with those beautiful eyes, covered in pain and helplessness, as he walks back. He gently cups her face and tilts it up, taking a deep breath as he sees the marks left.

"Here, place this around your neck; the marks should be gone in an hour or two. Rest; I'll be back soon."

"Thank you."

She looks at him like he's everything to her, and he wants that to be true so bad, but he knows it's not. He turns to leave, but she grabs his arm.

"Wait, Shadow." He pauses, turning back. He lifts an eyebrow, questioning her.

"I know you are going to carry the guilt of me getting hurt, so I am telling you right now, don't. It wasn't your fault; it was mine for letting the mother live." She looks at him, staring him down. She sees him flex his hand as he tries to calm down. He looks at her and nods, and she lies down to sleep.
