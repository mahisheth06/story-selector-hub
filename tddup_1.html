Till Death Do Us Part

By Mahi Sheth


She walks through the gates of Hell, into a pit of darkness, exhausted from her latest assignment. She hears the screams from the tortured souls just outside the palace. Their screams sound like cries for help. Lightning flashes across the sky, and Hell lights up. Death stops in place as she sees them reach their shackled arms toward her. Their faces are unrecognizable as the soul-eating parasites devour their essence. Death walks alongside the middle, careful not to touch the punished, toward the castle. The day in Hell had just begun, but telling it by the sky was impossible. It was pitch black all day and all night. Thunder was heard around Hell, its lighting flashing occasionally.

Reaching the double doors, black snakes slither across the surface, poised to strike at any moment at an unknown threat. Their scales are covered in venom, potent enough for a drop to obliterate a soul. Death pulls the handle and braces herself as she opens the door. Hellfire blasts out in streams, the heat feeling like the scorching sun against her face. Death steps into her home, only to be flung against the wall near the doors, an invisible grip holding her neck against the hard stone surface.

“HOW DARE YOU DISOBEY ME ONCE AGAIN?! The Devil’s voice shatters the silence of the palace, and Death freezes in fear as her eyes widen in terror.

“FATHER, ENOUGH! HOW DARE YOU RAISE YOUR HAND AGAINST MY WIFE!” Death looks over towards the sound. Shadow stands at the top of the stairs, his hands full of shadows, sending them toward Lucifer.
