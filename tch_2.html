Page Two of

The Cursed Heirs

By Mahi Sheth


She expected shock, maybe betrayal. But instead, the boy met her gaze with a defiant glare. “I know exactly who she is.” His voice was steady, unwavering. “Just like I know what that mark on your face means.” His eyes flicked to the dark crimson streak pulsing beneath her skin, threading through the veins near her eyes. “Your family should have perished in the battle.”

Her smirk vanished. Shadows flickered in her gaze as her fingers tightened into a fist. In one swift motion, she grabbed his arm and yanked him away from the girl.

“Careful how you speak to me, boy.” Her voice was low, venomous. “I was going to be merciful—kill you both quickly. But now?” She leaned in, her breath cold against his skin. “Your families will suffer, just as mine did.” With a sharp wave of her hand, the shadows coiled around him, rising from the floor like living chains, locking him in place.

“You don’t touch her. She’s a child. She has nothing to do with this.” He said, as he struggled against the binds.

Ignoring him, she walked over to the little girl, who slept soundly, so innocent and carefree. It’s what her childhood should have been like. Not one of pain and suffering. Not one of starvation and hatred.

As she lifted her hand, placing it on the child’s forehead, the air stilled before sudden sharp gusts of wind whipped around them, carrying voices from the past, distant and broken, like echoes trapped in time. Some hissed with venomous intent, others were ragged, horse with agony. Her eyes glowed with blue flames flickering like restless embers, dancing and shifting as she voiced the curse to take place.

“Your blood, once noble and pure, shall become a mark of ruin, a shadow cast upon all who claim it. No matter how much they may wish to love you, your presence will bring only destruction. Every heart that dares to belong to you will wither. You will be the harbinger of their fall, the traitor born of the sins of those who came before you. Your very existence will forever poison the throne, cursed by the actions of your ancestors long before your birth. This is the price of their betrayal, Princess. A heart that can never be loved, a future that can never be yours. Let this curse be the chains that bind you to your fate—one that will be spent in darkness, hidden away from those who once should have called you daughter. And know this—only when blood of another kingdom runs alongside yours, and the throne you long to inherit is claimed by a hand not your own, will you find a way to break what was forged today, and grant yourself freedom. But beware, for it is a price not all will be willing to pay.”
